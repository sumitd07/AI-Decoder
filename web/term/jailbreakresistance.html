Jailbreak resistance

▲ Rising

Also called Robustness to Jailbreaks

How well a model’s safety training holds when users get creative about tricking it into breaking the rules.

Think of it like

A bouncer who does not fall for "I left my ID in the car" or any of the fifty other lines they hear a night.

Example

A model refuses a harmful request even when it is wrapped in a role-play, translated to another language, or hidden inside a fake "developer mode" prompt.

How it actually works

Jailbreaks exploit the gap between a model’s helpfulness and its rules, using framing, obfuscation, or persona tricks to route around refusals. Resistance comes from safety fine-tuning plus separate classifier layers, but it is an arms race — new jailbreaks appear constantly. Measured as attack success rate over a red-team suite, ideally trending down.

For product teams

A safe-in-the-demo model is not safe if a Reddit thread can talk it out of its guardrails by Friday.

For engineers

Track attack success rate across jailbreak families; harden with safety fine-tuning plus an independent constitutional classifier.
